Pollutri The Undiscovered Treasure

Welcome to Pollutri. One of the hidden gems of the Abruzzo region in central Italy. Here you can experience scenic landscapes, medieval architecture, taste great food and some of the best wines at affortable prices that Italy has to offer, and we will give you all the information that you need to discover everything that Pollutri has to offer.

Beginning

Around 568, the hill on which Pollutri now stands was covered with thick forests and there were only a few huts of shepherds and farmers. The Lombards, soon arrived and did their utmost to integrate with the indigenous population. The village was expanded so much that in 580 it had already become very important and its territory reached to the sea, bounded by the Sinello Osento rivers. The name Pollutri derives from a temple dedicated to Pollux.

Modern Times

Today Pollutri has a a population of approximately 2500, and is situated in a small hill surrounded by vineyards and various fields. It has typical medieval Italian architecture and is 10km from the Adriatic. Life moves slowly here and it is the perfect place for relaxed a vacation.

What can I do in Pollutri?

Pollutri has a couple of very good restaurants, one of which is especially good, the excellent "Pizzeria Kappa". Around Pollutri there are several wine producers, who very often organize wine tasting events. It is also situated very near to the sea, so swimming is also an option and of course, 30km away, is the fantastic San Vito, with it's beautiful marina and the traditional trabocchi fishing platforms. If you are ready for a whole day excursion, Pompei and Naples are also a few hours of driving away. There is always something to do and see in Pollutri!
